BASIC STATISTICAL CONCEPT

*POPULATION: This can be defined as the entire group of individuals or cases that you are interested in studying.

* SAMPLE: This can be defined as the entire or representative part of the population, This is a subset of individuals or cases selected from the population.

* VARIABLE: This is a characteristic or attitude that is being measured or observed.

* DATA: This are the actual values of measurements obtained from the sample or population.

* PARAMETER: This is the numerical value that describes the characteristics of a population.

* STATISTIC: This is the numerical value that describes the characteristics of a sample.

* STATISTICS: This is defined as the science of Collection, Presentation, Analysis and Interpretation of numerical data.

COLLECTION OF DATA

This is the process of gathering information from various sources for the purpose of making an enquiry, Study or Research.

TYPES OF DATA

1. Primary Data
2. Secondary Data

1. Primary Data: This are data collected by the investigator himself for the purpose of specific enquiry or Study.
Such data is original in character and has never been used by anybody.
Primary data can be collected by the following methods.

i. Direct Personal Interface
ii. Indirect Oral Interface
iii. Schedule Sent Through Enumerators
iv. Mailed Questionaire

i. Direct Personal Interface: This is a situation were an investigator personally meets and informat inorder to get information or collect data.

ii. Indirect Oral Interface: Under this method the investigator contacts witnessess, neighbours, or third parties, friends to supply necessary informations. This method is preferred if the required information is on addiction, theft, cause of fire, murder.

iii. Schedule Sent Through Enumerators: Under this method, enumerators or interviewers take list of questions to meet the informants and fill their replies based on the question asked.

iv. Mailed Questionaire: Under this method, a list of questions are prepared and sent to a responded to (informer) by post. This list of questions is called a Questionaire. A covering letter explaining the purpose of investigation is sent to the respondent. Essense request the respondent to fill the blank spaces provided and return some within a specific time. This method is appropriate where the informants are literate.

CHARACTERISTICS OF A GOOD QUESTIONAIRE

- Number of questions should be minimum or few.

- Questions should be in logical order from easy to difficult.

- Questions Should be made short and simple. Technical terms are vague expressions should be avoided.

- Questions should be capable of having precised answers e.g Yes or No.

- Questions should be carefully framed so as to capture the entire scope of the survey.

- Questions which require calculations should be avoided.

- Questions should be attractive and sufficient space should be provided for answering questions.

- The wordings of the question should be proper without hurting feelings or arousing resentment.

2. Secondary Data: This are data or existing data collected from other sources. it refers to data that has already been collected and used by others, such as published research papers and articles, government reports and statistics, online data basis and social media platforms, Academic and institutional reports.

PRESENTATION OF DATA

Presentation of Data refers to the process of organizing, summarizing and displaying and data in a clear and meaningful way to facilitate understanding and analysis. This can be done using Plot, Charts, Graphs, Measures of Central Tendencies and Variabilities, Tables, Frequency distributors.

INTERPRETATION OF DATA

Interpretation of Data involves Identifying pattern and trends and drawing conclusions based on the data.
